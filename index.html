Instructions

Use this planner to record your completed subjects (passes and credits), see which subjects you can choose from for the coming semester, and develop your own timetable.

  1. Step 1: Choose your major from the dropdown at the top of the sidebar.

    By your 2nd semester you should decide which of the Network Security, Business Analysis, and Software Development streams will be your major. If you make a switch beyond then, your graduation might be delayed.

    If you are in your first semester, you can leave it as "Undecided". It will show Network Security as the major and you can make a different decision next semester.

  2. Step 2: Enter your pass and credit history.

    When you do, they will be marked darker in the grid, and the subjects available to you now (because of satisfied prerequisites) will change.

    Two buttons are available for this:

    • By clicking to mark completed subjects by clicking in the grid.
    • By typing to type or paste subject codes (e.g. BIT111 or USE101). Spaces, commas, subject names, and extra text do not matter; the subject codes will be pulled out.

  3. Step 3: Select your timetable by clicking subjects. Three ways to do this:

    1. Clicking in the main grid.
    2. Clicking on a subject in the sidebar below "Available now".
    3. Clicking on the "Available Now" button in the sidebar.

    You are expected to choose 4 subjects, which is a "full load". You can vary your load by pressing the Change button.

    Domestic students can study 1, 2, 3, or 4 subjects each semester.

    International students must normally study a full load of 4 subjects (visa requirement). This can only be varied in exceptional circumstances such as a high failure rate that led to an academic intervention.

    Studying more than 4 subjects is only possible when 9 or fewer subjects remain in the final year or 5 or fewer subjects remain in the last semester.
